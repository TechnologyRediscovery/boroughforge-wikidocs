Printing

Made by Natalie Merola with Scribe



1. Start on a code enforcement landing page.

Tip: Tip! You will be able to print different types of letters or permits, but the overall process is very similar across all printable documents.

2. Here are letters types and templates.

3. Because I am using a lightening case as the example, I already have an NOV drafted for me. If you made a regular case, the you would have to select New Letter, then generate an NOV for those violations.

4. Click "Preview draft" to look at the text of the letter before you finalize.

5. Here you can see the sample text.

6. Click "edit letter text" to make any changes.

7. Click "Choose addressee"

8. Click "Select person"

9. Select "Mail NOV to this address" linked under "Address links".

10. Click "Done! Return to NOV setup"

11. Click "select a notifying officer user"

12. Select whomever is sending the letter.

Tip: Tip! The notifying officer must have a phone number and email in the system for this to work!

13. Edit any text if you have to.

14. You can remove certain violations if you need to.

15. Click "Update notice text" to save the draft.

16. Click "finalize and lock".

Alert: Alert! After you select "finalize and lock" you cannot change the letter text. If you need to amend something, you will have to create a new draft.

17. Click "record mailing". This will be added as an event for the case.

18. Click this text field to enter a number of days until the system reminds you about the NOV.

19. Click "Mark as mailed!"

Alert: Alert! Only mark something as mailed of you plan to mail it out THAT DAY. Occasionally, the date of when a letter was mailed will come up in court. The saved event will help you remember the date. However, accurate information when it comes to court testimony is very important.

20. Click "Print"

21. Here is the final copy.

22. Printing will look different based on the web browser that you're using. For Google Chrome, look to the top right corner of the screen and click the three vertical dots. Find "print". Make sure that there are no headers or footers on the document. Selecting "more settings" will allow you to reconfigure the document as you would like it before printing.
